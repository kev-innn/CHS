What is Leather?

Leather is a popular material in the fashion industry and is used for making a wide range of products such as jackets, shoes, belts, wallets, handbags, and furniture upholstery. It is appreciated for its timeless appeal, durability, and natural charm. The process of creating leather involves several steps. First, the raw animal hide or skin is obtained, typically from cows, sheep, goats, or pigs. This raw material undergoes a process called tanning, which stabilizes the proteins and prevents decay. Tanning can be done using different methods, with the most common ones being vegetable tanning and chrome tanning.
